# Friday scorecard — the four gate numbers

Every Friday afternoon before close, about 10 minutes. These four numbers are how Mark steers the business — a photo of the sheet is all he needs.

## Fill in the sheet

1. Get this week's scorecard sheet. [MARK: confirm where the blank sheets are kept]
2. Fill in the **four gate numbers** for this week:

- **New patients + how they heard about us** — count of new patients, and the "How did you hear about us?" answer for each one
- **Average frame discount %** — this week's average discount given on frames
- **Jenny win-back** — letters/SMS sent · bookings made · patients who attended
- **Recall check** — did the recalls actually send this week? Yes or no, plus roughly how many

3. Get each number from Optomate or the appointment book. [MARK: confirm the exact Optomate report names for each of the four numbers]

> IF a number looks way off (about double or half of normal): add a sticky note saying why (school holidays, someone on leave, a big order) before you photograph it. A weird number with no note causes an hour of head-scratching on Monday.

## Photograph and save

4. Lay the sheet flat in good light and photograph the **whole page** — all four numbers readable.
5. Save the photo into the scorecard drop folder on this computer: `C:\Claude\Claude Code\Claude Second Brain\scorecard-drop` [MARK: confirm how the photo gets from the phone to that folder — email to yourself, OneDrive, or cable]
6. That's it. Claude reads that folder early Monday morning and Mark sees the numbers in his weekly brief.

> If Friday gets away from you, the sheet can go in over the weekend — it just has to be in the folder before Monday morning.
